## Introduction

Images are one of the most widely shared forms of content on the internet. Every day millions of photos are uploaded to social media platforms, messaging apps, websites, and online news platforms. Because images can quickly capture attention and communicate information visually, they are often trusted more easily than text.

However, modern editing tools and artificial intelligence have made it easier than ever to manipulate images. With just a few clicks, a photo can be altered to change its meaning completely. These manipulated images can be used for misinformation, harassment, scams, and even identity impersonation.

Understanding how images are manipulated online is important for recognizing fake content and protecting oneself from digital deception. It also highlights the importance of verification platforms such as Sniffer, which analyze images to determine whether they have been altered or generated using artificial intelligence.

## Why Image Manipulation Is Increasing

There are several reasons why image manipulation has become more common in recent years.

**First**, photo editing software has become extremely accessible. Applications such as Photoshop, mobile editing apps, and AI-based tools allow users to modify images easily without advanced technical skills.

**Second**, artificial intelligence has significantly improved image generation and editing capabilities. AI models can now generate realistic faces, remove objects from photos, or replace backgrounds automatically.

**Third**, social media platforms allow images to spread quickly. A manipulated image can reach thousands or even millions of viewers before anyone verifies its authenticity.

Because of these factors, manipulated images have become a major challenge in digital media.

## Common Image Manipulation Techniques

There are several techniques used to manipulate images online.

### Face Replacement

One of the most common techniques involves replacing a person's face with someone else's face. This is often done using artificial intelligence models trained on facial images.

Face replacement is frequently used in deepfake images and videos. In some cases, it is used for entertainment or social media filters, but it can also be used maliciously to create fake or embarrassing content involving another person.

Verification platforms such as Sniffer can analyze facial inconsistencies and digital artifacts to identify whether an image may contain face manipulation.

### Object Removal

Another common technique involves removing objects or people from an image.

For example, a photo editor can erase a person from a group picture or remove a background element to change the context of the image. Advanced editing tools can fill in the missing area so that the change becomes difficult to notice.

Although object removal may appear harmless in some situations, it can be used to manipulate evidence or misrepresent events.

### Image Splicing

Image splicing involves combining elements from multiple images into a single image.

For instance, someone may take the background from one photo and combine it with a person from another photo. If the editing is done carefully, the resulting image may appear realistic even though the scene never actually existed.

Image splicing is often used in misinformation campaigns or fake news to create misleading visual narratives.

### Lighting and Color Manipulation

Adjusting lighting and color settings can significantly change how an image appears.

By modifying brightness, contrast, shadows, and color balance, an editor can create dramatic effects that alter the emotional impact of a photo. In some cases, these adjustments can be used to make an image appear more threatening or misleading.

Because lighting adjustments are subtle, detecting them often requires digital forensic analysis rather than simple visual inspection.

### Artificial Intelligence Image Generation

Recent advances in artificial intelligence have introduced a new form of image manipulation: **AI-generated images**.

AI models such as generative networks can create completely synthetic images that appear realistic. These images may depict people who do not actually exist or events that never happened.

Synthetic images are increasingly used for fake social media profiles, scams, and misinformation.

Platforms like Sniffer analyze visual patterns and digital fingerprints left by generative models to identify AI-generated images.

## Why Manipulated Images Are Dangerous

Manipulated images can cause serious consequences in digital society. They can be used to:

- Spread false information
- Damage reputations
- Create fake evidence
- Facilitate harassment or blackmail

For example, non-consensual image manipulation can place a person's face into inappropriate scenes without their permission. These images can spread rapidly online and cause emotional distress to the victims.

Because of these risks, it is important to verify the authenticity of images before sharing or trusting them.

## How Image Verification Works

Image verification involves analyzing digital signals within a photo to determine whether it has been altered.

Forensic analysis may include examining:

- **Metadata** — embedded information about the image file
- **Pixel-level patterns** — statistical anomalies in pixel data
- **Compression artifacts** — traces of JPEG or other compression methods
- **Lighting inconsistencies** — unnatural shadows or highlights
- **Color gradients** — abnormal color transitions or banding

Advanced verification systems combine several detection methods to produce a more accurate authenticity assessment.

Platforms such as Sniffer integrate multiple detection techniques, including metadata analysis, artifact detection, and provenance verification. By analyzing these signals together, the system can determine whether an image is likely authentic or manipulated.
